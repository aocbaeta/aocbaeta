## Featured Project: Honeycomb BdG Quantum

A scientific computing project for studying superconductivity and spin-orbit coupling in honeycomb lattices using the Bogoliubov-de Gennes formalism.

The project is inspired by my previous research in condensed matter physics and extends those ideas computationally through numerical simulation, symbolic modeling, and exploratory quantum-computing mappings.

The project implements an 8x8 BdG Hamiltonian with sublattice, spin, and Nambu degrees of freedom, including s-wave and p + ip superconducting pairing, intrinsic spin-orbit coupling, Rashba interaction, minimum-gap analysis, and phase-map generation.

It also explores reduced quantum-computing mappings with PennyLane, Qiskit, and Cirq, connecting classical numerical simulation with quantum algorithms such as VQE.

**Technologies:** Python, NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, SymPy, PennyLane, Qiskit, Cirq.

## Related Project: commute

A symbolic computation tool for operator algebra in second quantization, focused on commutators, anticommutators, and algebraic expansions used in quantum many-body and field-theoretical calculations.

The project is directly related to my previous undergraduate research on the equations of motion for Green's functions, where symbolic computation was used to automate calculations involving quantum operators and many-body Hamiltonians.

The goal is to support formal manipulations commonly used in quantum mechanics, many-body theory, quantum field theory, condensed matter physics, superconductivity, and perturbative expansions.
